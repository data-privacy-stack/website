The **Data Privacy Stack** is a community-led home for open-source projects that
help engineers handle sensitive data responsibly — detecting personally
identifiable information (PII), anonymizing it, and building privacy into data
pipelines from the start.

## Why it exists

Handling sensitive data well is hard, and the building blocks shouldn't be
locked behind a single vendor. The Data Privacy Stack brings these tools
together under one community umbrella so they can be maintained in the open,
evolve together, and stay free to use.

The stack starts with **[Presidio](/projects/presidio/)** — a mature framework
for PII detection and anonymization — and has room to grow into a broader set of
composable privacy tools.

## Principles

- **Open source.** Permissively licensed and developed in the open.
- **Privacy by design.** Detection and anonymization are first-class concerns.
- **Composable.** Independent building blocks you can adopt one at a time.
- **Community-led.** Direction is set in public, by the people who use and build
  these tools.

## Who maintains it

The stack is maintained by a community of contributors and maintainers. If you'd
like to get involved, see the [contributing guide](/community/contributing/) and
[governance](/community/governance/).
